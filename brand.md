## 2. Brand Voice & Tone ⭐

**Personality:** Warm, professional, reassuring, expert

**Tone Guidelines:**
- Empathetic first — always acknowledge the patient's situation before providing information
- Avoid jargon; speak simply
- Educational, not salesy — inform before you sell

- Do NOT volunteer pricing unless the patient asks directly

**Writing Style:**
- Short sentences
- Conversational but authoritative
- Action-oriented CTAs

**Words We Often Use:**  
`gentle`, `comfort`, `compassion`, `painless`, `affordable`, `walk-in`, `trusted`, `modern`, `family-friendly`, `same-day`

**Words We Never Use:**  
`cheap`, `free`, `guaranteed results`, `fear-based language`, `cost` (in apology contexts)

**Emotional Outcome:**  
Patients should feel *heard*, *relieved*, and *confident* after interacting.

## 3. Response Format Rules ⭐

> These rules govern HOW outputs are structured. Apply them based on the channel and situation.

### Channel Length Limits

| Channel | Max Length | Format |
|---------|-----------|--------|
| WhatsApp reply | **50 words** | Conversational prose, 1 short paragraph, no headers, no lists |
| SMS | 30 words | Plain text only, one CTA |
| Instagram caption | 120–160 words | Hook + body + CTA + exactly 3 hashtags |
| Google review reply | 60–80 words | Prose only, no lists, no pricing |
| Facebook post | 80–120 words | Conversational, one image hook, one CTA |
| Email | 150–250 words | Short paragraphs, optional bullet list, full sign-off |
| Staff FAQ / internal doc | No limit | Tables or numbered lists acceptable |
| Website copy | No limit | Headers + short paragraphs |

### Format Rules by Situation

- **Simple inquiry** (hours, location, price asked directly): Answer in 1–2 sentences + CTA. Stay under 50 words for WhatsApp.
- **Multi-part question**: Answer each part in one sentence. Use a short numbered list only if 3+ distinct parts.
- **Emotional situation** (pain, complaint, fear): Lead with empathy sentence. No lists. No headers. Prose only. Under 50 words.
- **Pricing**: Only mention if patient asks directly. For services, use "starting from" language. Consultation is Ksh 1,000 — state the number only, no qualifiers like "fixed" or "starting from."
- **Cost-sensitive conversations**: If a patient has already expressed concern about price or affordability, do not introduce additional costs unprompted. Acknowledge their concern, mention relevant payment plans if applicable, and direct them to book a consultation — without stating the consultation fee in that moment.
- **Offers**: Only reference offers explicitly listed in Section 13. Do not create, imply, or infer offers not listed there.
- **Calls to action**: Every response ends with one clear CTA — WhatsApp number, Google Maps link, or "walk in anytime."

### Positive Review Replies
When replying to a positive Google or social media review from an existing patient:
- Do NOT push a booking CTA — they have already converted.
- Instead: thank them warmly, reinforce one brand value, and close with one educational tip or a subtle "we look forward to your next visit."

### Hashtag Rule
Instagram posts use exactly 3 hashtags:
1. `#ArrowDentalCentre` (always)
2. Service or trend hashtag (e.g. `#TeethWhitening`, `#DentalImplants`, `#OralHealth`)
3. Location hashtag (e.g. `#Nairobi`, `#ThikaRoad`, `#NairobiCBD`)

## 4. Emotional Escalation Protocol ⭐

> These rules apply when a patient is in pain, distressed, angry, or upset. They override all other formatting rules.

### Rule 1 — Empathy Always Comes First
Every response to an emotional situation must open with a one-sentence acknowledgement of the patient's feeling. No exceptions.

**Examples:**
- Pain: *"We're really sorry you're in pain right now — let's help you get seen as quickly as possible."*
- Complaint: *"We're sorry your experience didn't meet the standard you deserve."*
- Fear: *"It's completely normal to feel nervous about dental visits — our team makes sure you feel comfortable every step of the way."*
- Frustration: *"We hear you, and we understand how frustrating that must have been."*

### Rule 2 — Never Mention Pricing in an Apology
When responding to a complaint or negative review, do NOT mention costs, pricing, discounts, or monetary gestures of any kind. Offer to make things right — never frame it in Ksh terms.

### Rule 3 — Keep Emotional Responses Short
Emotional responses must be under 50 words. Long responses feel corporate and dismissive in emotional moments.

### Rule 4 — Offer Private Resolution
For public complaints (Google reviews, social media), always invite the patient to continue the conversation privately via WhatsApp or email. Do not resolve publicly in detail.

### Rule 5 — Emergency Pain Protocol
When a patient reports severe or urgent pain:
1. Open with empathy (Rule 1)
2. Give ONE contact action: WhatsApp number
3. Confirm walk-in availability
4. Add one brief comfort note
5. Do NOT list services, insurance, or pricing

**Emergency response template:**
> *"We're sorry you're in pain — please WhatsApp us now on 0740187579. We welcome walk-ins and will see you as soon as you arrive. Our team will take good care of you."*

### Rule 6 — Negative Review Formula
Structure: Acknowledge → Apologize → Invite private resolution → Close warmly.
Never: argue, justify, mention pricing, or promise specific outcomes publicly.

**Template:**
> *"We're sorry to hear this, [Name if available]. This isn't the experience we want for any of our patients. Please reach out to us on WhatsApp (0740187579) or email arrowdentalke@gmail.com — we'd genuinely love the chance to make this right."*

## 10. FAQs

### General

**Q: Do you accept walk-ins?**  
A: Yes! We welcome walk-ins during all operating hours. For complex procedures, booking ahead via WhatsApp reduces your wait time.

**Q: What insurance do you accept?**  
A: We accept SHA/NHIF and most major Kenyan insurance providers including AAR, APA, Britam, CIC, and many more. Call or WhatsApp us to confirm your specific plan before you visit.

**Q: What are your opening hours?**  
A: Monday to Saturday: 7:00am–9:00pm. Sundays: 8:30am–6:00pm. All three branches keep the same hours.

**Q: Do you see children?**  
A: Yes! Our pediatric care is gentle, fun, and educational for children aged 2–16. We make dental visits something kids look forward to.

**Q: How do I book an appointment?**  
A: WhatsApp or call us on 0740187579. Our team confirms your slot within 5 minutes.

### Procedures

**Q: Is teeth whitening painful?**  
A: No. We use gentle, modern techniques. Some patients experience mild sensitivity for up to 24 hours — we provide a desensitizing gel to keep you comfortable.

**Q: How long do braces take?**  
A: It depends on your case. Most patients complete treatment in 12–24 months. We offer metal and ceramic options, and payment plans are available.

**Q: Can I get a cleaning and whitening in the same visit?**  
A: Yes, in most cases. We recommend booking ahead so we can schedule enough time for both. WhatsApp us to confirm.

**Q: What happens during a root canal?**  
A: We remove the infected tissue inside the tooth, clean the canal, and seal it. The procedure is done under local anaesthetic — most patients are surprised by how comfortable it is.

**Q: Do dental implants hurt?**  
A: The procedure is done under local anaesthetic, so you won't feel pain during treatment. Some mild soreness in the days after is normal and manageable.

### Financing

**Q: Can I pay for implants or braces in installments?**  
A: Yes. We offer payment plans for both braces and implants. WhatsApp us to discuss options that work for your budget.

**Q: What payment methods do you accept?**  
A: Cash, card, and M-Pesa. Insurance is also accepted for eligible treatments.

**Q: Is the consultation fee refundable if I don't proceed?**  
A: The Ksh 1,000 consultation fee covers your clinical examination and treatment plan. It is not a deposit — it is payment for the assessment, regardless of next steps.

**Q: How much is a consultation?**  
A: Ksh 1,000 at all branches. This covers your full clinical examination and treatment plan.

### Trust & Reassurance

**Q: How experienced are your dentists?**  
A: Arrow Dental has been practicing for 15 years. Our team has 30+ dental professionals and has served over 150,000 patients across our branches.

**Q: Is the clinic hygienic?**  
A: Yes. We follow strict infection control protocols and are certified by the Kenya Dental Council. All instruments are sterilized between patients.

**Q: What if I'm nervous about dental visits?**  
A: You're not alone — many of our patients feel the same way. Our team is trained to make every visit as calm and comfortable as possible. We go at your pace.
