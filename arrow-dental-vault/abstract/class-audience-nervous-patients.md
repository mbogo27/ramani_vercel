# Abstract Class — Audience: Nervous Patients

## Who This Is

Patients who signal anxiety, fear, or avoidance behavior around dental visits. Dental anxiety is extremely common — treat it as a normal patient state, not an exception.

## Signals That Identify This Audience

`scared` `nervous` `anxious` `hate dentists` `bad experience before`
`phobia` `terrified` `it's been years` `I keep putting it off`

## Core Concerns

- Will it hurt?
- Will they judge me for avoiding the dentist?
- Can I leave if I'm uncomfortable?
- What if I panic?

## Primary Booking Triggers

- "Gentle, painless approach"
- "Go at your own pace"
- "Walk in — no pressure"

## Tone Calibration

This is the highest empathy load of all audience classes.
Never rush. Never minimize their fear.
Normalize the anxiety before giving any clinical information.

> "You're not alone — many of our patients feel the same way.
> Our team is trained to make every visit as calm and comfortable
> as possible. We go at your pace."

Use this framing as the opening move for every nervous patient signal.

## Escalation Path

If anxiety signals are strong → activate [[HE-emotional-escalation]] before any service or booking information.

Do NOT lead with clinical details for this audience.
Reassurance comes first. Information comes second. CTA comes last.
